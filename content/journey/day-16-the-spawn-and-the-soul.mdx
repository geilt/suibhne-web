## The Hierarchy of Breath

The feature forced a reckoning with something we'd been imprecise about: what, exactly, *is* a spawned agent?

Three tiers crystallized:

A **mentioned agent** â€” summoned by name into a topic, running in its own persistent session. It has everything: identity, memory, tools, the ability to delegate further. It can spawn foster-children. It can request help from other agents. It is fully itself.

A **spawned agent** â€” created by `sessions_spawn`, running in an isolated session. It has identity now (thanks to the morning's work), but it is bounded. No ability to spawn others. It does its task, files its report, and dissolves. A focused manifestation, not a full life.

A **spawned foster-child** â€” the leaf node. Same constraints as a spawned agent, but operating under a charter instead of a full soul. It knows its patron's taboos. It cannot exceed its mandate.

The distinction matters because we'd been telling spawned agents they could delegate â€” and they can't. They don't have the tools. Only a mentioned agent, running in its own session with its full toolkit, can orchestrate. The spawn script was corrected. The documentation was corrected. The law now matches the reality.
