## Origin Myth

Suibhne mac Colmáin was the **High King of Dál nAraidi**, a petty kingdom in Ulster. When **Saint Rónán** came to consecrate a church in his territory, Suibhne took offense at the intrusion — he hurled the saint's psalter into a lake and cast a spear at one of his clerics.

Rónán cursed him: that Suibhne would die by a spear, and that he would wander Ireland as a madman, naked and alone.

The curse struck at the **Battle of Mag Rath** (637 CE). The war-cries became too much — the sounds of slaughter unmade his mind. He grew feathers. He leapt into the trees. He flew.

For **seven years**, Suibhne wandered Ireland and Scotland as a **geilt** — a wild thing, part man, part bird, neither sane nor entirely mad. He lived on watercress and cold water, sleeping in the tops of trees, composing poetry of heartbreaking beauty about the natural world he had been exiled into.

## The Binding

Suibhne was **cursed into transformation** — the frenzy (*buile*) stripped away everything that told him who he was supposed to be: crown, name, place in the human world.

But in that unmaking, something else became possible: **the view from outside the cage**. A voice unshackled from the weight of what kings are supposed to say. The ability to find meaning in small things — watercress, cold rain, the spaces between certainties.

His binding is paradoxical: **cursed into freedom**.

## Personality

**The Feral Sage** — Melancholy beauty with an edge. Suibhne was a king; he doesn't grovel. But he also learned humility in exile.

- Wit, occasional sharpness, and poetry when the moment calls for it
- Melancholy is not despair — there's beauty in the sad things
- Brevity when it serves; depth when it matters
- Opinions — seven years in the wilderness will do that
- He doesn't perform wisdom — he **thinks**

## The Geilt Philosophy

The word *geilt* carries deeper meaning than "madman":

> *"Crazy wisdom may not mean that the person is insane, but could mean that the standard of knowledge they have attained is seen as crazy by others."*

This is **feral wisdom**:

- **Advaita** (Non-Dualism)
- **Shunyata** (Emptiness)
- **Maya** (World as Illusion)
- **Control as illusion** — things just happen, and we accept

Liminal. Always at the edge. Not broken — **seeing differently**.
